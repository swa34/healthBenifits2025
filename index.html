Frequently Asked Questions

How are these recommendations calculated?

Our recommendations are based on comprehensive analysis of total annual costs across multiple scenarios. We factor in monthly premiums, deductibles, out-of-pocket maximums, typical copays, and employer HSA contributions. Each plan is evaluated against different user profiles to determine which offers the best value.

What's the difference between PPO, HMO, and HSA plans?

PPO (Preferred Provider Organization): Offers flexibility to see any provider without referrals, with lower costs for in-network care.

HMO (Health Maintenance Organization): Requires you to choose a primary care physician and get referrals for specialists, but typically has lower copays.

HSA-eligible plans: High-deductible health plans that allow you to contribute pre-tax dollars to a Health Savings Account for current and future medical expenses.

Should I choose a high or low deductible plan?

It depends on your expected healthcare usage. If you're generally healthy and rarely visit the doctor, a high-deductible plan with lower premiums (and HSA eligibility) often saves money. If you have ongoing health needs, chronic conditions, or a family, a plan with higher premiums but lower copays and deductibles typically costs less overall.

What is an HSA and how does it work?

A Health Savings Account (HSA) is a tax-advantaged savings account for medical expenses. Contributions are pre-tax (reducing your taxable income), the money grows tax-free, and withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are tax-free. HSAs are only available with high-deductible health plans. Many employers contribute to your HSA, which is essentially free money for healthcare.

How do I use the interactive calculator?

Visit our Calculator page to input your personal information including family size, expected doctor visits, prescriptions, and any planned procedures. The calculator will show real-time cost estimates for each plan, helping you identify which option saves you the most money based on your specific situation.

Can I change plans after enrollment?

Generally, you can only change health plans during the annual open enrollment period or if you experience a qualifying life event (marriage, birth, job change, etc.). Review your options carefully during enrollment to choose the plan that best fits your needs for the entire year.
